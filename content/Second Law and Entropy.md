# Second Law and Entropy

The **second law** answers *which* energy transfers are physically allowed, not just how energy is balanced ([[First Law of Thermodynamics|first law]]).

## Clausius statement

Heat cannot spontaneously flow from a colder body to a hotter body without external effects.

## Kelvin–Planck statement

It is impossible to construct a device that operates in a cycle and produces no effect other than the absorption of heat from a single reservoir and the performance of an equal amount of work.

Equivalently: no heat engine can be **100% efficient**.

## Entropy

Entropy $S$ is a state function defined through a reversible path:

$$
\mathrm{d}S = \frac{\delta Q_\text{rev}}{T}
$$

For an **isolated** system:

$$
\Delta S_\text{isolated} \geq 0
$$

Equality holds only for reversible processes.

## Entropy generation

In general:

$$
\mathrm{d}S = \frac{\delta Q}{T} + \mathrm{d}S_\text{gen}, \qquad \mathrm{d}S_\text{gen} \geq 0
$$

## Connection to engines

The [[Heat Engines and Carnot Cycle|Carnot cycle]] achieves the maximum efficiency allowed by the second law between two reservoirs at $T_H$ and $T_C$:

$$
\eta_\text{Carnot} = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H}
$$
