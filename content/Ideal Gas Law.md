# Ideal Gas Law

An **ideal gas** is a model where molecules do not interact and occupy negligible volume.

## Equation of state

$$
PV = nRT = mR_s T
$$

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| $P$ | absolute pressure |
| $V$ | volume |
| $n$ | amount of substance (mol) |
| $R$ | universal gas constant, $8.314\ \mathrm{J\,mol^{-1}K^{-1}}$ |
| $m$ | mass |
| $R_s$ | specific gas constant |
| $T$ | absolute temperature |

## Internal energy of an ideal gas

Joule's law: $U = U(T)$ only. Therefore for any process of an ideal gas:

$$
\mathrm{d}U = m c_v\,\mathrm{d}T
$$

Combine with the [[First Law of Thermodynamics|first law]] to relate $Q$, $W$, and $\Delta T$.

## Isothermal compression

At constant temperature, $\Delta U = 0$, so $Q = W$ and:

$$
W = \int_{V_1}^{V_2} P\,\mathrm{d}V = mRT \ln\!\left(\frac{V_1}{V_2}\right)
$$

The figure shows an isotherm ($T = \text{const.}$) and an adiabat ($Q = 0$) on a $P$–$V$ diagram.

## Specific heat relations

For an ideal gas:

$$
c_p - c_v = R_s
$$

and

$$
\gamma = \frac{c_p}{c_v}
$$
