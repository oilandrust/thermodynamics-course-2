# First Law of Thermodynamics

The **first law** is a statement of energy conservation for thermodynamic systems.

## Closed system energy balance

For a closed system undergoing a process from state 1 to state 2:

$$
\Delta U = U_2 - U_1 = Q_{1 \to 2} - W_{1 \to 2}
$$

where:

- $U$ — internal energy
- $Q$ — heat added **to** the system
- $W$ — work done **by** the system

> Sign convention used here: heat in and work out are positive.

## Differential form

At an instant:

$$
\mathrm{d}U = \delta Q - \delta W
$$

The notation $\delta$ reminds us that $Q$ and $W$ are path-dependent quantities, unlike the state function $U$.

## Work at a moving boundary

For quasi-static expansion against pressure $P$:

$$
W = \int_{V_1}^{V_2} P\,\mathrm{d}V
$$

See the pressure–volume diagram in [[Ideal Gas Law#Isothermal compression]].

## Example: heating a gas at constant volume

If $W = 0$ then $\Delta U = Q$. For an [[Ideal Gas Law|ideal gas]] at constant volume:

$$
Q = m c_v \Delta T
$$
